F1 in Schools Car Manufacturing Optimization

This project focuses on enhancing the production process of a miniature Formula 1 car for the F1 in Schools competition by integrating sustainable manufacturing techniques. By transitioning from traditional subtractive methods to advanced additive manufacturing—primarily Stereolithography (SLA)—the design ensures high precision, minimal material waste, and improved aerodynamic performance.

Project Description

The F1 in Schools program challenges students to design and build model Formula 1 cars. Traditionally, these are fabricated using CNC and laser cutting, which produce excessive material waste. This project introduces a revised design and manufacturing workflow centered on additive manufacturing, particularly SLA, using PETG resin. This change improves the car’s aerodynamic capabilities, surface finish, and structural integrity while also promoting sustainable practices in STEM education. The project includes designing the car body, structural frame, and assembly using a blend of SLA and machined aluminum parts, with a production layout developed using Systematic Layout Planning (SLP).

Design Requirements

Lightweight and aerodynamically optimized car body High precision and smooth surface finish Durable, corrosion-resistant structural components Efficient and cost-effective manufacturing process Minimal post-processing and material waste Adaptability to educational settings and small production scales.

Problem-Solving

The project addresses the inefficiencies and environmental impact of traditional subtractive manufacturing. Additive Manufacturing (AM) via SLA allows complex geometries, improved accuracy, and a smoother surface finish with reduced waste. PETG was selected for its impact resistance and minimal warping during printing. Structural parts like the chassis, requiring greater strength, are machined from 304 aluminum. Systematic Layout Planning (SLP) is used to optimize workflow, reduce movement, and integrate post-processing and inspection.

Final Result

The final model car features an SLA-printed PETG aerodynamic body and aluminum-machined chassis. Key components include SLA-fabricated spoilers, wheel mounts, and accessories, integrated with ball bearings (606Z) and bonded using epoxy adhesives. The assembly process ensures precise alignment and functional integrity. The production facility follows a compact, U-shaped layout equipped with desktop SLA printers, post-processing stations, and automated material handling systems, making it ideal for educational use and scalable manufacturing.
