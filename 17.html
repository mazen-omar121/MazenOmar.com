Internship

As part of a research internship at Universiti Teknologi Malaysia (UTM) under Dr. Aminuddin Bin Saat and in collaboration with PETRONAS, I contributed to the development of a smart system to monitor and predict corrosion occurring beneath insulation in steel pipelines. This system integrates various sensors—including optical fiber, thermocouples, and humidity/pressure sensors—into a modular test rig that emulates real-world industrial conditions. My work spanned research, prototyping, IoT coding, app development, and lab testing using advanced equipment like Fiber Bragg Grating (FBG) sensors and spectrum analyzers. The goal was to establish a cost-effective, sustainable, and accurate monitoring system to mitigate industrial CUI challenges.

Project Description

The CUI project at UTM aims to provide a sustainable and efficient method for monitoring and predicting corrosion in insulated steel pipes used in industrial applications. The focus is on simulating realistic operational conditions through a closed-loop water circulation rig while integrating cutting-edge sensors and IoT-based data transmission. Key technologies include Fiber Bragg Grating (FBG), BME280, DHT11, thermocouples, and optical fibers coated with polyetheramine for high sensitivity to environmental changes. This project is a collaboration between UTM and PETRONAS to improve safety, performance, and longevity of critical infrastructure.

Design Requirements

Develop a modular, sustainable corrosion testing rig with realistic industrial insulation. Select and integrate sensors for pressure, humidity, and temperature both inside and outside insulation layers. Enable wireless data acquisition through an IoT platform for real-time monitoring. Code and deploy a system to collect and visualize sensor data through a user-friendly app. Ensure insulation system is easy to assemble/disassemble for efficient testing and data collection.

Problem-Solving

The biggest challenge was predicting corrosion before it occurred by identifying measurable precursors. I conducted extensive research on industrial insulation conditions and collaborated with experts to understand the behavior of pressure, humidity, and temperature on corrosion. I received training in the Laser Lab to handle delicate optical fiber sensors and learned to use spectrum analyzers. Selecting the right materials for rig construction required engagement with the MRCG Lab and iterative testing to combine PU and mineral wool effectively. Designing the IoT system posed its own challenges, which I solved by leveraging Arduino Uno Rev 4 WiFi and crafting custom code to link all sensor inputs and transmit data seamlessly.

Final Result

The final prototype includes: A closed-loop rig with a water tank, pump, and pipe system insulated using PU and mineral wool. Embedded sensors (FBG, BME280, thermocouples, DHT11, optical fibers) to monitor environmental parameters inside and outside insulation. A modular insulation casing designed for easy replacement and reconfiguration. An IoT-enabled Arduino system with cloud-based data transfer to a custom app for real-time display. A complementary HTML/CSS website for AsiaPlus as part of additional project work. This system offers a scalable solution for industrial applications, capable of reducing maintenance costs and preventing failures caused by undetected corrosion under insulation.
